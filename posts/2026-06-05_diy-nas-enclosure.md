# DIY NAS Part 1: The Enclosure

#NAS
#project


*This is part 1 of the project, see [here](/diy-nas-plan) for the other parts.*

I thought I would 3d print the HDDs mounts, but it all ended up being too large for my small printer. I opted instead to
order a pair of 6mm Aluminium plates. This had the added benefit of acting as a heat sink, not that I'm expecting
sleeping drives to get hot.

I took my time marking it all out [precisely and accurately](/precision-vs-accuracy). It helped that I found an [official spec](https://support.wdc.com/images/kb/2579-810188-000_P02_2.pdf)
from Western Digital for the placement of the mounting screws so didn't need to rely on taking my own measurements.

![](/images/diy-nas-hdd-mount-spec.png)

I stuck both plates together with double-sided tape to ensure that the holes in the plates would align with one another.
It also meant drilling half as many holes. Next time I'll use much less tape, I had to heat the plates up to well above
100C before it let go.

I was very pleased, surprised even, at how well it turned out. All the screws went in without any trouble.
